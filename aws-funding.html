The differentiation

What most billing software cannot do, and we can

Each of these is uncommon or absent in mainstream billing and RCM tools. The market notes reflect our 2026-07-16 vendor survey (Waystar, Availity, Optum/Change, pVerify, Tennr, GenHealth, and the programmable-clearinghouse reference).

AI first pass, human confirms

AI fills the likely answers and flags every field it touched; the biller confirms, and it is logged.

Market note: mainstream tools are still manual entry or static rules. Agentic prefill-with-confirmation is rare.

Out-of-network recovery and IDR

NSA clocks, negotiation, and arbitration (the Arbitron module) recover dollars others write off.

Market note: mainstream RCM targets in-network; our survey found no major vendor tuned for 99%-OON specialty work.

Predictive pre-submission scrub

Ranks what is missing, proposes the fix, and pre-flights the clearinghouse's own edits before the claim leaves.

Market note: most scrubbers are static checklists that only tell you what is wrong.

Coding assist from the report

Reads the clinical report to catch billable CPT codes that would otherwise be missed.

Market note: few billing tools read the clinical document to find revenue.

Prior authorization automation

Detects whether auth is required, requests and tracks it, assembles medical necessity, lands the number on the claim.

Market note: still largely manual and portal-driven across the industry.

Document understanding

Turns faxes, tech reports, and insurance cards into structured claim data automatically.

Market note: a capability clients currently rent from separate AI vendors.

Programmable and agent-operable

API-first with its own MCP server, so AI agents and partners can drive the system, not just a closed screen.

Market note: most billing software is a closed UI with no programmable surface.

One AI-native platform, full cycle

Intake, scrub, file, auto-reconcile the remittance, and arbitrate, event-driven and multi-tenant.

Market note: the cycle is usually stitched from several disconnected tools.
